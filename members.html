  • I am interested in quantum gravity and cosmology. My research focuses on the deep role that quantum information plays in these settings. According to the holographic principle, which I helped develop, the geometry of spacetime is closely related to its information content. This relation presents us with sharp puzzles, such as the black hole information problem. The AdS/CFT correspondence is a concrete quantum gravity theory in which some of these questions can be studied in detail. Meanwhile, we have begun to exploit the connection between information and geometry to discover new, provable features of relativistic quantum field theory, such as the quantum null energy condition. It also has overlap with condensed matter physics and has led to applications in current quantum computing platforms. I lead GeoFlow, a multi-institutional consortium of theorists and experimentalists working at the interface of these areas.

  • Grant's research interests are at the nexus of quantum field theory, quantum gravity, general relativity, particle physics, and cosmology. His recent work includes proving the weak gravity conjecture, bounding quantum corrections to effective field theories, new area theorems in general relativity, reformulations of graviton perturbation theory, cosmic inflation, testing ideas for emergent spacetime, and investigating the holographic relationship that connects spacetime geometry with quantum entanglement.

  • My research focus is centered around quantum field theory and quantum gravity. I am currently interested in understanding entanglement in QFT using algebraic methods such as Tomita-Takesaki theory, as well as defect CFT methods. I am particularly interested in the connection between these ideas and black hole thermodynamics, specifically in understanding its boundary description within AdS/CFT. I have also worked on using covariant phase space techniques to study the “edge mode” degrees of freedom associated to null boundaries, including dynamical event horizons.

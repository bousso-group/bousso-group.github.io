  • Ning Bao

    Ning’s research interests lie at the intersection of quantum information theory and quantum gravity. In particular, his current research focus lies on applying notions of entanglement measures, quantum channels, quantum random walks, and relative entropies to the study of the AdS/CFT correspondence and other related generalizations.

  • Grant Remmen

    Grant's research interests are at the nexus of quantum field theory, quantum gravity, general relativity, particle physics, and cosmology. His recent work includes proving the weak gravity conjecture, bounding quantum corrections to effective field theories, new area theorems in general relativity, reformulations of graviton perturbation theory, cosmic inflation, testing ideas for emergent spacetime, and investigating the holographic relationship that connects spacetime geometry with quantum entanglement.

  • Chris Akers

  • Ven Chandrasekaran

    My research focus is centered around quantum field theory and quantum gravity. I am currently interested in understanding entanglement in QFT using algebraic methods such as Tomita-Takesaki theory, as well as defect CFT methods. I am particularly interested in the connection between these ideas and black hole thermodynamics, specifically in understanding its boundary description within AdS/CFT. I have also worked on using covariant phase space techniques to study the “edge mode” degrees of freedom associated to null boundaries, including dynamical event horizons.

  • Illan Halpern

    Illan’s research in classical and quantum gravity investigates the fundamental structure of spacetime and its connections to information theory.

  • Arvin Shahbazi-Moghaddam

    My current focus is on applying information theoretic objects to quantum field theories, in particular finding relations between energy and entanglement entropy. I’m also very interested in studying the puzzles associated to black holes in quantum gravity, with the goal of figuring out whether old black holes have interiors.

  • Vincent Su

    I enjoy thinking about problems at the intersection of quantum gravity and quantum information. In particular, I’m interested in applications of (quantum) computation in better understanding holography.
